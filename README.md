## Questions Answered

### Business Analyst — Financial & Portfolio
- **D-01** Which films delivered the highest WW box office, and what does the top-5 Pareto concentration reveal about portfolio dependency?
- **D-02** How has production budget inflation tracked across four eras — and what has happened to ROI as budgets grew?
- **D-03** How does the ratio of original IP to franchise films shift across eras, and what does that signal about creative risk appetite?
- **D-06** How has the domestic vs international revenue split shifted — and what is the growing international dependency risk?
- **DG-01** Why do films budgeted $92–175M achieve higher average ROI than the $200M ceiling? Returns curve or selection effect?
- **DG-02** Why did the Cars franchise decay (RT: 75→40→69) while Toy Story maintained quality across four entries?
- **DG-04** What caused Era 4 underperformance — streaming policy, COVID, quality decline, or audience habit shift?
- **DG-06** Is international revenue growth driven by Disney's distribution scale, storytelling choices (Coco), or macro cinema growth?
- **P-01** Based on historical budget-to-revenue regression, what is the expected WW range for a new $175M original in 2025?
- **P-02** If Pixar greenlights a Coco sequel, what is the probability of outperforming the original — based on historical sequel base rates?
- **PR-01** Should Pixar implement a hard $175M budget cap for original IP? What governance process for exceptions?
- **PR-02** What data-driven sequel greenlight scorecard should be mandated before any franchise is approved?
- **PR-03** What decision matrix should govern theatrical vs Disney+ release — with clear financial thresholds?
- **PR-06** What 5-year genre and franchise portfolio strategy should Pixar adopt based on the data?

### Marketing Analyst — Audience, Brand & Earned Media
- **D-04** What is the CinemaScore distribution — and how does it differ between franchises and originals?
- **D-05** Which films show the largest divergence between critic scores and audience scores — and what does that reveal?
- **DG-03** What is the mechanism linking CinemaScore A+ to higher box office — opening weekend, legs, or downstream IP value?
- **DG-05** Why do Oscar winners accumulate 2–4× more IMDb votes over a decade — what is the earned media mechanism?
- **P-03** Can Rotten Tomatoes pre-release scores predict CinemaScore grades as a leading indicator for marketing spend decisions?
- **P-05** What is the predicted 10-year audience reach multiplier from a Best Animated Feature Oscar win?
- **PR-04** What pre-release marketing and test-screening strategy maximises the probability of a CinemaScore A+ grade?
- **PR-05** How should Pixar restructure its awards campaign investment as a multi-year brand asset strategy?
- **PR-07** What minimum theatrical run threshold justifies a film's production budget — and how should this shape release timing?

## Key Findings

- **Top-5 concentration is 35.8%** of total $17B revenue — healthier than Pareto 80/20 predicts, but every top-5 film is a franchise sequel. No original IP has ever broken the top 5.
- **Budget sweet spot is under $100M** with 774% avg ROI. The $200M ceiling averages 352% — driven by franchise entries masking poor original IP returns at that level.
- **Era 4 streaming releases cost an estimated $1.6–2.4B** in foregone theatrical revenue. Soul (RT:95), Luca (RT:91), and Turning Red (RT:95) all exceeded the portfolio's RT average — their underperformance was distribution policy, not quality.
- **CinemaScore A+ films earn $56M more on average** than A-grade films and significantly outperform on second-weekend retention, which compounds across the theatrical window.
- **Oscar winners accumulate 2.6× more IMDb votes** than non-winners over their lifetime — quantifying the long-tail audience value of awards as brand investments, not one-time PR events.
- **Coco (2017) is the strongest unactivated sequel candidate**: RT:97, IMDb:8.4, WW:$815M, CinemaScore:A+, 74% international revenue share — passes every criterion of the greenlight scorecard.
